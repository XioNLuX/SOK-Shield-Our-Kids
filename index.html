Our Mission

Shield Our Kids (SOK) is dedicated to preventing school shootings and creating safer educational environments for all children. We believe that through early intervention, community awareness, and evidence-based prevention strategies, we can stop violence before it happens.

Our comprehensive approach addresses the root causes of school violence while building resilient communities that support every student's mental health and wellbeing. We work with schools, families, and communities to create environments where warning signs are recognized, help is accessible, and every child feels valued and supported.

Digital Monitoring Guide for Parents

Modern parenting requires digital awareness. Learn how to effectively monitor your child's online activity, recognize concerning digital behaviors, and maintain trust while ensuring safety in today's connected world.

üì± Smartphone Monitoring Basics

Essential monitoring strategies for mobile devices:

Built-in parental controls:

  • iPhone: Screen Time settings - app limits, content restrictions, communication safety
  • Android: Family Link by Google - activity reports, app approval, location tracking
  • Both platforms: Check screen time reports weekly, review app usage patterns

Third-party monitoring apps:

  • Qustodio: Comprehensive monitoring across all devices
  • Norton Family: Web filtering, time supervision, location tracking
  • Bark: AI-powered content monitoring for concerning messages
  • Circle Home Plus: Network-level filtering for all connected devices

What to monitor:

  • App downloads and usage time
  • Web browsing history and search terms
  • Text messages and instant messaging apps
  • Social media posts and interactions
  • Photos and videos taken/received
  • Location data and movement patterns

üõ†Ô∏è Technical Implementation Guide

Step-by-step setup for effective monitoring:

Transparent monitoring approach:

  • Explain why monitoring is necessary (safety, not distrust)
  • Involve teens in setting up monitoring systems
  • Regular family meetings about digital safety
  • Earn trust through consistency and fairness
  • Gradually reduce monitoring as trust and maturity increase

When to increase monitoring:

  • After discovering concerning content or behaviors
  • During mental health crises or depression
  • Following disciplinary issues at school
  • When friends or teachers express concerns
  • During major life transitions or family stress

üíª Computer and Internet Monitoring

Home computer safety and monitoring:

Router-level controls:

  • DNS filtering: Use OpenDNS or CleanBrowsing for automatic content blocking
  • Router settings: Access your router admin panel to set time restrictions
  • Guest networks: Put kids' devices on separate network with more restrictions

Computer monitoring software:

  • Windows: Built-in Microsoft Family features + third-party tools like Spyrix
  • Mac: Screen Time + third-party options like Kidslox
  • Chromebook: Family Link integration for school devices

Browser monitoring:

  • Check browser history regularly (kids often delete, so use router logs)
  • Install browser extensions like BlockSite for content filtering
  • Review bookmarks and saved passwords
  • Monitor downloads folder and recent file access

üéÆ Gaming Platform Monitoring

Gaming presents unique risks - here's how to monitor:

Console parental controls:

  • PlayStation: Family Management settings - spending limits, communication restrictions
  • Xbox: Microsoft Family settings - screen time, content filters
  • Nintendo Switch: Nintendo Switch Parental Controls app

PC gaming monitoring:

  • Steam: Family View mode, purchase restrictions
  • Discord: Most concerning app - monitor voice/text chat carefully
  • Epic Games: Parental controls in account settings

Warning signs in gaming:

  • Secretive about who they're playing with
  • Using voice chat with strangers
  • Receiving gifts or money from online "friends"
  • Dramatic mood changes after gaming sessions
  • Mentions of meeting online friends in person

üö® Red Flags in Digital Activity

Concerning digital behaviors to watch for:

Immediate concern indicators:

  • Researching weapons, violence, or mass shooting events
  • Joining extremist groups or forums
  • Creating detailed plans for violence
  • Expressing hatred toward specific groups or individuals
  • Sharing or creating violent content

Behavioral changes:

  • Suddenly becoming very secretive about device usage
  • Quickly switching screens when you approach
  • Using devices only in private spaces
  • Deleting history or using incognito mode excessively
  • Creating multiple social media accounts

Communication patterns:

  • Receiving messages from unknown adults
  • Engaging in conversations about violence or revenge
  • Discussing depression, suicide, or self-harm
  • Planning to meet online contacts in person
  • Sharing personal information with strangers

‚öñÔ∏è Balancing Privacy and Safety

Maintaining trust while ensuring protection:

Age-appropriate monitoring:

  • Ages 6-10: Full supervision, limited independent access
  • Ages 11-14: Monitored access with clear rules and consequences
  • Ages 15-17: Graduated independence with spot-checking and trust-building
  • 18+: Respect privacy while maintaining open communication

Building trust through monitoring:

  • Be transparent about what you're monitoring and why
  • Involve teens in creating family digital agreements
  • Reward responsible behavior with increased freedom
  • Have regular check-ins about online experiences
  • Respect their need for some privacy while maintaining safety boundaries

When monitoring reveals concerns:

  • Don't panic - approach with curiosity, not accusation
  • Have calm conversations about what you found
  • Focus on safety and support, not punishment
  • Seek professional help if needed
  • Adjust monitoring level based on risk assessment

Know the Signs

Early identification of concerning behaviors can save lives. Understanding warning signs, monitoring mental health, and knowing when to seek help are critical skills for parents, educators, and community members.

‚ö†Ô∏è Immediate Warning Signs

Seek immediate help if you observe:

  • Direct threats - Verbal or written threats to harm others
  • Detailed plans - Specific discussions of violence or revenge
  • Weapon acquisition - Seeking access to firearms or other weapons
  • Rehearsal behaviors - Practicing attacks or violent scenarios
  • Final acts - Giving away possessions, saying goodbye
  • Fixation on violence - Obsession with mass violence events
  • Timeline pressure - Mentions of specific dates or deadlines
If any of these signs are present, contact law enforcement immediately. Do not wait.

üìä Behavioral Changes to Monitor

Watch for significant changes in:

  • Social isolation - Withdrawing from friends and activities
  • Academic performance - Sudden decline in grades or attendance
  • Sleep patterns - Insomnia, nightmares, or excessive sleeping
  • Appetite changes - Significant weight loss or gain
  • Hygiene neglect - Sudden lack of self-care
  • Mood swings - Extreme emotional fluctuations
  • Increased aggression - Unusual anger or violence toward people/animals
  • Risk-taking - Reckless or dangerous behaviors

üß† Mental Health Red Flags

Signs of potential mental health crisis:

  • Depression signs:
    • Persistent sadness or hopelessness
    • Loss of interest in previously enjoyed activities
    • Feelings of worthlessness or excessive guilt
  • Anxiety indicators:
    • Excessive worry or fear
    • Panic attacks or physical symptoms
    • Avoidance of normal activities
  • Concerning thoughts:
    • Obsessive thoughts about violence or revenge
    • Paranoid beliefs or conspiracy theories
    • Grandiose plans or unrealistic thinking

üí¨ How to Check Your Child's Mental Health

Regular mental health check-ins:

Create safe conversation spaces:

  • Schedule regular one-on-one time without distractions
  • Ask open-ended questions: "How are you feeling lately?"
  • Listen without judgment or immediate solutions
  • Validate their emotions, even if you don't understand

Monitor digital activity responsibly:

  • Be aware of social media posts and interactions
  • Notice changes in online behavior or friend groups
  • Watch for concerning content consumption
  • Balance privacy with safety concerns

Stay connected with their world:

  • Know their friends, teachers, and activities
  • Attend school events and maintain teacher contact
  • Be aware of academic and social pressures
  • Notice changes in peer relationships

üéØ Risk Factors to Understand

Factors that may increase risk:

  • Personal grievances - Real or perceived injustices
  • Social rejection - Bullying, exclusion, or romantic rejection
  • Family stress - Divorce, abuse, financial problems
  • Academic failure - Poor grades, disciplinary actions
  • Substance abuse - Alcohol or drug use affecting judgment
  • Access to weapons - Unsecured firearms in the home
  • Media influence - Obsession with violent content or mass shooters
  • Mental health issues - Untreated depression, anxiety, or other conditions

Important: Having risk factors does not mean someone will become violent. These are indicators to watch more closely and provide additional support.

üÜò When and How to Get Help

Immediate action required:

  • Call 911 for immediate threats
  • Contact school administration about school-specific threats
  • Report concerning online activity to appropriate platforms
  • Remove access to weapons immediately

Professional help options:

  • Primary care physician - First step for medical evaluation
  • Mental health professionals - Therapists, counselors, psychiatrists
  • School counselors - Immediate support and resources
  • Crisis hotlines - 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline
  • Hospital emergency rooms - For acute mental health crises

Support the process:

  • Accompany your child to appointments
  • Follow through with treatment recommendations
  • Maintain open communication with providers
  • Create a supportive home environment
  • Consider family therapy if recommended

üîç Trust Your Instincts

If something feels wrong, don't ignore it. Parents, teachers, and community members often notice changes before others do. Your observations and concerns matter.

Document concerns
Keep records of concerning behaviors, statements, or changes you observe
Share information
Communicate with other adults in the child's life (teachers, coaches, relatives)
Act on serious concerns
Don't wait for "proof" - err on the side of safety and seek professional guidance

Firearm Safety & Secure Storage

Proper firearm storage is one of the most effective ways to prevent school shootings, teen suicides, and accidental deaths. Every gun owner has a responsibility to secure their firearms from unauthorized access.

üîí Essential Storage Guidelines

ALWAYS follow these critical safety measures:

  • Double-lock system - safe + trigger lock minimum
  • Hide all keys and combinations - children are resourceful
  • Teach gun safety rules:
    • Stop - don't touch
    • Don't touch - leave it alone
    • Leave the area immediately
    • Tell an adult right away
  • Regular safety conversations - age-appropriate discussions
  • Monitor for depression/anger - extra vigilance during crises

üè† Home Security Best Practices

Comprehensive home firearm security:

  • Gun safes: Biometric, combination, or key-locked
  • Quick-access boxes: For home defense (adults only)
  • Ammunition storage: Separate locked container
  • Regular inventory: Know exactly what you have
  • Secure from visitors: Friends, relatives, workers
  • Travel security: Locked cases for transport
  • Insurance considerations: Coverage for theft/damage

‚ö†Ô∏è Warning Signs & Prevention

When to take extra precautions:

  • Mental health crises - temporary removal from home
  • Domestic violence situations - immediate secure storage
  • Substance abuse issues - enhanced security measures
  • Teen behavioral changes - depression, anger, isolation
  • Threats of self-harm - remove access completely
  • School disciplinary issues - increased monitoring

Remember: It's better to be overly cautious than to face tragedy.
